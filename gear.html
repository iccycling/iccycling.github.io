ICCC Club Wear

From high-quality racing kits to hoodies and casual wear, ICCC blends the performance edge of road cycling brands like MAAP or Pas Normal Studios with the laid-back vibe of a college club. Our range is designed so you look and feel great — not just in competition or on group rides, but also on the way to lectures through Hyde Park and South Kensington. We offer more than just full race kits, and we’re always open to ideas for new club wear. If you’re interested, reach out — we’re happy to share tips on where to find quality cycling gear at a fair price, whether it’s new or second-hand. Check our blog for a beginner’s guide to saving on cycling equipment, or explore affordable gems on platforms like Vinted for top-notch pre-loved kit.

Storing bikes in London can be challenging due to limited space. Many students have to bring their bikes into their rooms because secure storage options are often unavailable. This makes urban cycling tricky, and many opt to use Santander or Boris Bikes instead. It’s important to check if you can store your bike in a courtyard or basement, or if it’s allowed to keep it in your room. For sport bikes, indoor storage is usually fine, and they can even become a stylish accessory. For everyday bikes, bringing them indoors can be difficult because of dirt and water from rain. Expensive sport bikes aren’t ideal for daily use — carbon forks can get damaged, and theft risk for costly bikes is very high in London. Imperial’s security team offers advice on locks, and the campus has locked bike parks. Overall, it’s best to use an inexpensive bike or public bike-sharing for everyday rides, and keep a separate bike for sport safely stored indoors. If storage options are limited, consider indoor cycling options the club offers or check with local bike shops about rental possibilities.
